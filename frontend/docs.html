Getting Started

Welcome to the AI LaTeX Resume Builder. Our goal is to make professional resume creation accessible to everyone using the power of LaTeX, without the steep learning curve.

What is AI LaTeX Resume Builder?

It's a web-based application that combines the professional typesetting of LaTeX with the simplicity of visual editing and AI automation.

Who should use it?

  • Job seekers looking for ATS-friendly resumes.
  • Students and Academics requiring formal CV structures.
  • Developers who want full control over their resume's code.

Using Templates

Templates are pre-designed LaTeX structures. They define the layout, fonts, and styling so you can focus on your content.

How to select a template

  1. Navigate to the Templates page from the top menu.
  2. Use the filters (ATS, Minimal, etc.) to find a style that fits your industry.
  3. Click "Use Template" on your preferred design.

What happens next?

After clicking "Use Template," you'll be taken to the Editor page with the template's code already loaded. You can then start replacing the placeholder text with your own experience.

Using the Editor

The Editor is your workspace. It's designed to give you a live feedback loop while you work.

Left Panel: LaTeX Source

This is where you type. You'll see text surrounded by commands like \section{...} or \item. Simply edit the text inside the curly braces.

Right Panel: Live Preview

This shows what your final PDF will look like. It updates whenever you click the "Recompile" button.

Compiling PDFs

In the world of LaTeX, "Compiling" is the process of turning your code into a visual document.

When to recompile?

You should recompile whenever you want to see how your changes look in the final design. Most users recompile after finishing a major section (like Experience or Education).

Common Errors

LaTeX can be picky about syntax. Here are the most common issues and how to fix them:

Missing Braces { }

Every opening brace must have a closing brace. If you delete one by accident, the compiler will get confused.

Undefined Commands

If you mistype a command (e.g., \sectoin instead of \section), you'll see an error message. Check your spelling!

Special Characters

Characters like & or % have special meanings. To use them as text, put a backslash before them (e.g., \&).

FAQ

Do I need to know LaTeX?

Not at all. You can simply edit the text within the templates. If you are comfortable with code, you can do much more, but it is not required.

Can I download without compiling?

No, because the PDF needs to be "built" from your latest changes first. Always compile one last time before downloading.

Are templates ATS-friendly?

Yes! We specifically tag templates that are optimized for Applicant Tracking Systems to ensure your resume gets through.

Is my data saved?

If you are logged in, your progress is automatically synced to the cloud. If not, your work remains in your browser until you close the tab.
